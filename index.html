üìä Executive Summary

This comprehensive analysis examines border killing incidents between Bangladesh and India by the Border Security Force (BSF) over a 52-year period (1971-2023). The study investigates the correlation between ruling political parties in both countries and the frequency of border incidents, providing critical insights into how political dynamics may influence border security outcomes.

üîç Key Insights & Findings

1. Political Correlation

The analysis reveals significant variations in border incident rates during different ruling party periods in both countries. This suggests that political dynamics and diplomatic relations may influence border security policies and outcomes.

2. Temporal Patterns

Clear temporal trends emerge across different political periods, indicating that border incidents are not random but may be influenced by specific policy changes, diplomatic relations, or security protocols during different administrations.

3. Bilateral Impact

The study demonstrates that political situations in both Bangladesh and India simultaneously affect incident rates, highlighting the bilateral nature of border security challenges and the importance of coordinated diplomatic efforts.

4. Normalized Analysis

By calculating average killings per year, the analysis provides normalized metrics that account for varying durations of political periods, enabling fair comparison across different ruling parties and timeframes.

5. Long-term Trends

The 52-year dataset reveals long-term patterns and cyclical trends that may be related to broader geopolitical shifts, economic conditions, or changes in border management strategies over decades.

6. Data-Driven Policy

This analysis provides evidence-based insights that can inform policy decisions, diplomatic negotiations, and border security management strategies to reduce incidents and improve bilateral relations.
